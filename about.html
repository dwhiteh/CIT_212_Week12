Aircraft Carrier

All current US aircraft carriers are powered by nuclear propulsion.

The US currently has 11 commissioned aircraft carriers.

Each with the capability to handle approximately 80 aircraft.

Arleigh Burke Destroyer

First commissioned on Independence Day 1991.

Capable of Anti-Air, Anti-Submarine, and Anti-Surface Warfare.

Holds a crew of over 300 personnel.

Ticonderoga Class Cruiser

Last Cruiser commissioned in 1994, 27 built in total and still active.

CG-57 named after the Battle of Lake Champlain.

Homeport out of San Diego. I am proud to have served aboard her.

Littoral Combat Ship

Capable of speeds in excess of 40 knots.

Designed by Lockheed Martin to operate in near-shore environments.

Two different hull types named Freedom and Independent.

Zumwalt Class Destroyer

Newest class of warship. Only three commissioned.

Significantly larger than Ticonderoga Cruisers and Arleigh Burke Destroyers.

Designed for stealth.
